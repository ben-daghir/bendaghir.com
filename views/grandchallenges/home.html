Invenergy A Grand Challenges Research Team

Our Vision

How might we reduce energy costs for low-income households through the use of thermochromic materials on exterior home surfaces?

Grand Challenges is a living and learning community focused on solving some of the world's prominant, multi-disciplinary, and multi-cultural problems.
My team of students focuses on the subdivision of energy. Our goal and vision is to provide lower-income families and individuals an increased access high standards of living while effectively lowering its cost. Our specified area of interest is climate control in homes, specifically through insulation. However, traditionally insulation is installed inside of the home as foam or fiberglass. What we propose, is a means of external insulation from solar radiation through the intrisic properties of colored paint.

How?

By utiliizing the properties of thermochromic paint that uses microencapsulation to create a lueco dye, the properties of color can be controlled through applied temperature. This allows for a passively adaptive paint to react to external environmental factos.

Has this been done?

The short answer is no. Lueco dye has typically been used in textiles and crafts, never as an insulator. However, there are a few studies and theses that have explored this capability, but most of the data is on a small scale and mostly theoretical. There are also some patents that reference this process; however, these patents were made to secure liscenesing deals, not to produce a working model. These patents expire in a few years, opening it up to the public domain. A detailed report on the research and patents can be found in our project proposal listed below.
